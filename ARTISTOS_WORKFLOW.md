# ArtistOS — Song Creation Workflow

## The BR CHIEF Workflow (Using ArtistOS)

---

## STEP 1: ARTIST PROFILE
**"Who is this artist?"**

Questions to answer:
- Artist name + brand identity
- Genre(s) — what kind of music?
- Target audience — who listens?
- Influences — who do they sound like?
- Persona — what's their story?
- Visual style — how do they look (faceless = silhouette, abstract, etc)

This is the FOUNDATION. Everything flows from who the artist is.

**BR CHIEF Example — Artist Profile:**
> Artist: BR CHIEF
> Genre: Southern Hip Hop, Trap, Club Banger
> Origin: Texas-born, Baton Rouge Louisiana raised
> Flow: Lil Wayne effortless + deep voice + grungy
> Influences: Webbie, Lil Boosie, Juvenile, Master P
> Persona: Bayou swagger, confident, street-wise, HBCU pride
> Visual: Faceless silhouette with bayou/neon aesthetic
> 
> **BR CHIEF Example — TIGER CITY (Song Concept):**
> "An anthem for Baton Rouge. Club banger that represents every hood in BR — from Scotland Hill to Eden Park. Grambling and Southern University pride. Tiger City represents unity and swagger."

---

## STEP 2: SONG CONCEPT
**"What is this song about?"**

Questions to answer:
- What vibe/mood? (hype, chill, emotional, party)
- What topic? (love, street life, celebration, struggle)
- What story are we telling?
- Who is the target listener?

---

## STEP 3: SONG STYLE
**"How does it sound?"**

Questions to answer:
- BPM range? (130-135 for club, 140 for hype)
- Key? (D minor for dark, etc)
- Genre? (Trap, bounce, hip-hop, R&B)
- Reference artists? (Webbie, Lil Boosie, Lil Wayne)
- Instrumentation? (808s, zydeco, hi-hats, synths)

**BR CHIEF Example — TIGER CITY:**
```
Style: Raw 808 + minimal synth + aggressive snare + hard hi-hats + subtle Zydeco undertone
BPM: 130-135
Genre: Hard trap drums, Louisiana street club anthem
Reference: Webbie/Lil Boosie energy
Mood: Dark club at 2AM, aggressive but confident
```

---

## STEP 4: WRITE LYRICS
**"What are the words?"**

Structure:
- Intro (0:00-0:15) — Door open, bass SLAMS in
- Hook (0:15-0:45) — The catchy repeatable part
- Verse 1 (0:45-1:20) — First story/point
- Hook Repeat (1:20-1:50) — Reinforce
- Verse 2 (1:50-2:25) — Second story/point
- Bridge (2:25-2:40) — Hype moment / crowd call-out
- Verse 3 (2:40-3:05) — Wrap up / big finish
- Outro (3:05-3:15) — Beat rides out / conversation

**BR CHIEF Example — TIGER CITY Hook:**
```
Tiger City! Where the real n*ggas stay
Every hood in BR, we run up in this place
Ova the hump, we done made it, now we here
Grambling to the G, we the ones they fear
```

---

## STEP 5: VISUALS & ANIMATION
**"What does it look like?"**

For faceless artists:
- Abstract/silhouette avatar (never shows face)
- Animated cover art (waveform, floating elements)
- Genre-specific visual style:
  - Club banger → dark/neon/laser effects
  - Chill → soft gradients/waves
  - Party → bright colors/confetti

**BR CHIEF Example — TIGER CITY Visuals:**
```
Art Style: Dark club aesthetic, neon tiger stripes, Louisiana flag colors
Animation: Pulsing waveform, floating "Tiger City" text, smoke effects
Thumbnail: Tiger silhouette with crown, BR flag in background
```

---

## STEP 6: PLATFORM DISTRIBUTION
**"Where does it go?"**

| Platform | Content Type | Goal |
|----------|-------------|------|
| **YouTube** | Full song video (3-5 min) | Ad revenue, virality |
| **YouTube Shorts** | 30-60 sec clip | Algorithm reach |
| **TikTok** | 30 sec teaser | Viral moment |
| **Instagram** | Reel with lyrics overlay | Fan engagement |
| **Spotify** | Full song | Streaming revenue |
| **Apple Music** | Full song | Streaming revenue |

**BR CHIEF Release Strategy:**
```
Day 1: YouTube (full video) + Spotify + Apple Music
Day 2: TikTok clip
Day 3: Instagram Reel
Week 2: YouTube Shorts (lyric snippet)
```
