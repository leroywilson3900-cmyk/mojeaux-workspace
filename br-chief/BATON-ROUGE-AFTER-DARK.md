## ALBUM OVERVIEW

**Album Title:** BATON ROUGE AFTER DARK
**Artist:** BR CHIEF
**Concept:** Baton Rouge at night - when the city comes alive. The clubs, the streets, the river, the boulevard, the people who make BR what it is after the sun goes down.

**Tagline:** *"Baton Rouge after dark. We don't sleep."*

**Core Vibe:**
PURE CLUB BANGERS. Every single track is high energy. Every BPM is 125 or above. Nothing slow. Nothing reflective. Nothing soft. BR Chief is DJing a party that doesn't stop for 8 straight tracks. You cannot play this album and stay still. Chopped-and-screwed undertone with maximum bounce. Club-ready hooks on every track. Every single track makes you move.

**What BR Means in This Album:**
- 🇺🇸 The Capital City (we got the State Capitol building on the river)
- 🐯 Tiger Town (BR is Tiger Town - LSU is part of it but SO MUCH MORE)
- 🌊 Mississippi River (BR sits RIGHT on it)
- 🌴 Bayou culture (swamp, cypress, alligators, humidity)
- 🍜 Louisiana food (gumbo, jambalaya, po'boys, crawfish)
- 🏙️ Nightlife (Perkins Road, the strip, the clubs, the energy)
- 💪 The grind (Capital City workers, LSU hospital workers, BR people working late)
- 🗺️ The geography (Florida Blvd, Northside, Southside, LSU, Mall of Louisiana, River Road)

## ALBUM STRUCTURE & NARRATIVE ARC

### ACT 1: THE ARRIVAL (Tracks 01-02)
**Energy:** High → Medium
The album opens with the club banger ("This Dancing After") - you KNOW this is BR. Then "Boulevard Nightlife" transitions into the cruising, late-night BR energy. Sets the vibe.

### ACT 2: THE REFLECTION (Tracks 03-04)
**Energy:** Medium → High
"Night Caller" is the slowdown - emotional, reflective, 2AM. Then "Tiger Town After Dark" brings the hype back - BR pride, tiger energy, unstoppable.

### ACT 3: THE ROOTS (Tracks 05-06)
**Energy:** Low → Medium
"Chopped & Screwed" is the deep, slowed homage to the Houston sound that shaped BR. Then "Dancing in the Lou" brings the party back - pure dancing, celebration.

### ACT 4: THE TAKEOVER (Tracks 07-08)
**Energy:** Medium → MAXIMUM
"Capitol Rebellion" is aggressive - BR Chief asserting dominance. Then "BATON ROUGE AFTER DARK" as the album closer - EVERYTHING. The full identity. The anthem. The whole city in one track.

## RELEASE STRATEGY

### PHASE 1: THE ANTHEM (Month 1)
**Track:** "THIS DANCING AFTER" (Track 01)
**Drop:** As music video + on all platforms
**Goal:** Get the BR CHIEF sound established - techno club + BR swagger + HANDLE IT DNA
**Content:** Music video shot on Florida Boulevard at night

### PHASE 2: THE STATEMENT (Month 2)
**Track:** "BOULEVARD NIGHTLIFE" (Track 02)
**Drop:** Audio + visual lyricizer
**Goal:** Show the breadth - BR bounce + nightlife energy
**Content:** Lyric video with BR night driving footage

### PHASE 3: THE IDENTITY (Month 3)
**Track:** "BATON ROUGE AFTER DARK" (Track 08 - the title track)
**Drop:** Music video
**Goal:** Full BR anthem, puts the city on notice
**Content:** Video featuring every part of BR - the Capitol, the river, Florida Blvd, LSU, the bayou

### PHASE 4: THE ALBUM (Month 4)
**Release:** Full album "BATON ROUGE AFTER DARK"
**Tracks:** 01-08
**Goal:** Complete body of work, BR Chief is HERE
